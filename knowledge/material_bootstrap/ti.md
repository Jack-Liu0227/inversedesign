# Titanium Alloy Design Bootstrap

## Typical Objective Patterns
- High strength plus usable ductility (for example: maximize UTS while keeping EL above threshold).
- Balance between Nb/Mo/Sn strengthening and retained plasticity.

## Composition Guidance
- Keep Ti as main balance element.
- Nb and Mo generally strengthen but can hurt elongation when excessive.
- Sn can improve strength/solid-solution behavior in moderate range.
- Avoid large random jumps in multiple alloying elements at once.

## Heat Treatment Method Guidance
- Use one complete route sentence only.
- Practical route pattern: solution treatment + quench + aging.
- Include temperature, holding time, and cooling mode in one sentence.

## Candidate Strategy
- Generate diverse but feasible compositions around known practical windows.
- Use small directional adjustments when previous candidates fail for ductility.
